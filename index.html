The Slumyard One: The First Edition


On 16 April 2021, COMETBOYS Entertainment made its debut with The Slumyard, held at Kitcheners Calvary Bar in Braamfontein, Johannesburg. The event’s theme, inspired by the slums of old South African townships and the 70s/80s style, was conceptualized by Siphiwe Molotsi. This collaboration between COMETBOYS and !PLAR set the stage for a unique and immersive experience that would become a hallmark of their events.


The Slumyard featured live performances from artists such as S.U.I, Witney., Blnkego, Colourful.Melancholy, and AURUM, alongside DJ sets by TNDO, Trinsik, Yolophonik, and DJ Dark Dindy. Though the event attracted just under 200 attendees, its impact was significant, generating word-of-mouth buzz that would fuel the success of future events.

The Slumyard Two: Fashion Week


Building on the success of the first event, COMETBOYS Entertainment hosted The Slumyard: Fashion Week on 18 June 2021 at Kitcheners Calvary Bar. The event merged fashion and music, drawing inspiration from renowned fashion weeks in New York and London. Attendees were encouraged to dress in their best outfits, and a photobooth was set up to capture these moments, creating an engaging and memorable experience.


The night featured performances from artists such as Elle Jaye, Washe, LLELLO, Don Cayla, and NZY, with DJ sets by The Journalist, Funi R, and DJ Sthezinbucks. The event attracted 278 people, marking a significant increase in attendance and solidifying COMETBOYS’ reputation for delivering high-quality events.

The Slumyard Three: Sports Day


On 16 October 2021, COMETBOYS hosted The Slumyard: The Sports Day, another innovative event inspired by high school sports days. Attendees were grouped into four "houses" with different colors: Green (Rock Whispers), Red (Tune Twisters), Yellow (Bass Bashers), and Blue (Melody Mentals). Each house supported performances by artists aligned with their color, creating a fun and competitive atmosphere.


Artists like Witney., LLELLO, Elle Jaye, serigiotheartist, Trinsik, and many others brought their energy to the stage, making this event a standout success with a turnout of 314 attendees.

The Slumyard Four: SLUM-MAS


After a brief hiatus, COMETBOYS returned with The Slumyard: Slum-Mus (Christmas Edition) on 26 November 2022. Held at Kwa B's in Maboneng, Johannesburg, this event embraced the festive season and attracted the largest audience to date, with 368 attendees. The performances by UNCOOL, Veenus, Unicade, wolfisdead, and others, alongside DJ sets by Trinsik, Yolophonik, and MHMMD, made the night unforgettable.

The Spin Off


In a departure from their usual format, COMETBOYS hosted The Spin Off on 19 February 2022 at Velvet Hour Cocktail Bar & Kitchen. Meet The Team


The COMETBOYS Entertainment team is made up of a dynamic trio, each bringing their unique strengths to the forefront of the brand’s success. Snegugu Mnguni, also known as MAJEED, is the co-founder and main event organizer, responsible for planning, coordinating, and executing all COMETBOYS events, while also handling data analysis and building vital industry networks. Minentle Ndzala, known as Blnkego, is the co-founder and financial strategist of the team—he oversees budgeting, captures purchases, and analyzes spending trends to ensure the company stays financially efficient. Wandile Matshebula, also called Trinsik, joined the team as a director and contributes a powerful creative vision, curating artistic elements and ensuring COMETBOYS maintains its cutting-edge cultural relevance. Together, they form a tight unit—each one a vital piece of the puzzle—working in perfect sync to grow the movement from the underground upward.

About COMETBOYS


Early Beginnings (2019 - Early 2020)


COMETBOYS Entertainment’s journey began in early 2019, with a shared vision among three individuals: Snegugu Mnguni, Minentle Ndzala, and Wandile Matshebula. They sought to create a platform that would bring music, art, and culture to life through unique and community-driven events. The idea for COMETBOYS was sparked through their involvement in local creative spaces and their desire to offer something fresh and exciting to Johannesburg’s entertainment scene.


Before the company was officially established in 2020, COMETBOYS collaborated with Afrimabonavision starting in December 2019. This early partnership helped the team gain experience and refine their approach to event management, laying a strong foundation for what was to come.


Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic and subsequent lockdowns throughout 2019 and early 2020, COMETBOYS continued to make progress. The pandemic did not hinder their drive or creativity. Instead, they used this time to strategize, plan, and prepare for the return of live events.



The Formation of COMETBOYS (2020)


By 2020, COMETBOYS Entertainment was officially established, with Snegugu Mnguni taking on the role of event organizer and networker, Minentle Ndzala handling the finances and trend analysis, and Wandile Matshebula joining as director in 2022, bringing his creative vision and deep understanding of the entertainment landscape to the team.


During this period, the trio was also involved in !PLAR, a creative partnership that consisted of Siphiwe Molotsi and Wandile Matshebula. Together, they worked on several projects, including conceptualizing themes for future COMETBOYS events. However, when !PLAR dissolved, Wandile fully transitioned to his role as director at COMETBOYS, while Siphiwe Molotsi became the creative director of COMETBOYS.
